# 1. Purpose

This document defines the Catalog business object of the Japan Sourcing Platform (JSP).

A Catalog is a curated collection of Products prepared by JSP for customer presentation, marketing activities, and inquiry generation.

Catalog is not a Product repository. It is a marketing-oriented presentation object.

---

# 2. Business Purpose

The Catalog object is designed to:

* Organize Products for customer presentation.
* Support marketing campaigns and business events.
* Support trade shows and exhibitions.
* Support seasonal promotions.
* Support online product browsing.
* Generate customer inquiries.

A Catalog represents a business presentation rather than a master data object.

---

# 3. Business Rules

## BR-001

A Catalog may contain multiple Products.

Relationship:

Catalog (1)

↓

Product (N)

---

## BR-002

A Product may belong to multiple Catalogs.

Relationship:

Product (N)

↓

Catalog (N)

---

## BR-003

A Catalog may contain Products from multiple Suppliers.

Supplier limitation does not exist.

---

## BR-004

A Catalog may be created before Products are assigned.

Empty Catalogs are allowed during preparation.

---

## BR-005

Every Catalog shall have one unique System Catalog ID.

Example:

CATLG-000001

The System Catalog ID is generated by the system and shall never be modified.

---

## BR-006

Catalog Name

Required:

* Japanese Name

Optional:

* English Name
* Chinese Name

---

## BR-007

Catalog Cover Image is required.

It is used as the primary presentation image.

---

## BR-008

Catalog Description is required.

It explains the business purpose of the Catalog.

---

## BR-009

Publication information

Catalog maintains:

* Published Date
* Updated Date

Optional:

* Expired Date

---

## BR-010

Catalog supports lifecycle status.

Examples:

* Draft
* Published
* Archived

---

## BR-011

Products inside a Catalog support Display Order.

Ordering is maintained independently for each Catalog.

---

## BR-012

Physical deletion is prohibited.

Catalog records shall be retired through lifecycle status management.

# 8. Design Principles

## Marketing-Oriented

Catalog exists for business presentation and customer communication.

It is not Product Master Data.

---

## Event-Driven

Catalogs are normally created for:

* Marketing Campaigns
* Trade Shows
* Seasonal Promotions
* Customer Collections

---

## Independent Product Ordering

Product display sequence belongs to Catalog rather than Product.

Different Catalogs may display the same Product in different positions.

---

## Multilingual Support

Japanese Name is mandatory.

English and Chinese names are optional.

---

## Logical Delete

Physical deletion is prohibited.

Lifecycle status shall be used instead.
